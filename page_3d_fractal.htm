Fractal Analysis

The fractal analysis consider the 3D space divided in cubes and counts the number of cubes that cover the cave (midline and splays). For a 3-dimensional object, when the cube side halves, ie is divided by 2, the number of cubes that cover the cave increase by 8 = 23. The fractal exponent at a given side is the power of 2 (ie, 3 in the example).

The fractal analysis finds this exponent for varying length of the cube side, and display the result as a graph.
