[11] СЕЧЕНИЯ

Поперечные сечения это что то особенное. Для того чтобы нарисовать поперечное сечение в TopoDroid вы должны нарисовать линию "сечения", либо на плане или на разрезе. Положенин линии сечения определяет положение поперечного сечения и его ориентацию.

Поперечное сечение это форма пещеры в плоскости, которая пересекает ее под прямым углом. Ориентация поперечного сечения является ориентация этой плоскости, то есть, направление линии, перпендикулярной к секущей плоскости и указывающей в направлении взгляда. Вы определяете ориентацию поперечного сечения тем как вы нарисуете линию сечения. В качестве индикатора направления линиия разреза имеет штрих, указывающий от линии по направлению взгляда поперечного сечения.
Размер этого "штриха" может быть изменен в меню Настройки.

Вы можете выбрать между фото поперечного сечения (снятого встройнной камерой) либо абрисом поперечного сечения. В первом случае вы просто делаете фотографию, которая напомнит вам о форме галереи, когда вы будете рисовать карту пещеры. Убедитесь в том, чтобы на картинке был спелеолог либо баночка от фотопленки , которые будут служить в качестве меры масштаба.

In the second case you sketch the cross section on a canvas with the usual drawing tools of TopoDroid. As a reference TopoDroid displays the leg shot traversed by the cross section and the splay shots at its endpoints. The station in front(the one that the cross-section is looking at) is centred in the grid. The splay shots in front are light grey, those behind are thinner darker grey. All the shots are displayed projected on the plane of the cross section. Once closed, cross-sections can be opened again and modified by selecting the relative section line in "edit" mode and tapping the Edit button.

A cross-section can be either "vertical" or "horizontal", depending on how the plane intersects the cave passage.

Cross-sections in the plan view
Cross-sections defined in the plan view are always "vertical" (zero inclination). Their orientation (ie, the azimuth of the cross-section plane) is determined by the angle of the section line. Very often they are perpendicular to the gallery, but can be drawn at any angle needed. For example, a section line drawn from left to right(e.g. horizontal, angle 90 degrees from the vertical) has a direction of view to the North (up, azimuth 0). If drawn slightly downwards at an angle of 120, it will have a direction of view to an azimuth of 30 degrees.
The crossed leg is displayed vertical when the cross-section has the same azimuth as the leg. Otherwise it is tilted right or left.

Cross-sections in the profile
Cross-sections in the profile view can be either "horizontal"(on vertical shots in pits) or "vertical"(on more or less horizontal galleries). They are considered "horizontal" if their inclination exceeds the value of the H-threshold setting.
The inclination is determined by that of the the section line.

"Horizontal" cross-sections are displayed always with the North up. Furthermore, a reference line 5 m long, oriented to the North, is added to them. The leg is shown at its azimuth
"Vertical" cross-sections always have the azimuth of the crossed leg (or the opposite one if the direction of the section line is the opposite of that of the leg). The crossed leg is displayed vertical as the cross-section has the same azimuth as the leg.

If the section line is traced from the right to left, the cross-section is oriented downwards and has -90 degrees inclination. This is a "horizontal" cross-section as seen from above. Most of the times you will want to draw "horizontal" cross-sections this way, as it is more natural to lay a map on a surface and look down at it. If you trace the section line from left to right the cross-section is oriented upwards (inclination +90 degrees).


At-station cross-sections [E]
The "Station" dialog, that you get by tapping on a station point in Edit mode, displays the station coordinates and has two or three buttons: <

  • "Continue the survey from here" sets the station as that to which the next shot will be attached
  • "Break midline display" marks (or unmarks) the station as a terminal point in the survey centerline data reduction
  • "X-Section" [E] opens a sketch window to draw a cross section at the station.
    • If the average shot clino is smaller than the "vertical threshold", the cross section at a station is drawn in a vertical plane passing through the station, otherwise it is horizontal. The cross-section at a station is useful when you take several splay shots around the station to describe the shape of the gallery. If there are two shots at the station the section plane is oriented along the bisectant of the angles formed by the (horizontal projections of the) shots. If there is only one shot it is oriented ortogonal to that. If there are more than two shots ToipoDroid cannot orient the section plane, and the button for the cross-section is inhibited.

    Cross-section files
    Each cross-section is saved in a separate Therion file in the "th2" subfolder. The files get consecutive names "MySurvey-xx0.th2", "MySurvey-xx1.th2", etc. The "xx0" numbering part of the name is also displayed in the Section dialog that pops up when you create or edit a section.
    The file is deleted when a cross-section is deleted.

    Cross-sections can be exported in other formats just like the other sketches, except in cSurvey format.
