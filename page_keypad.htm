TopoDroid Keypads

TopoDroid comes with a numerical keypad and a simple "qwerty" keyboard.

The numerical keypad is used for numerical values (lengths, angles, etc.). It has the ten digits, the decimal point, and the plus/minus sign. For the input of coordinates it has also the "degree" ('d') and "minute" ('m') keys.

The querty keyboard is used for station names. It has the ten digits, the 26 letters of the latin alphabet (lowercase and uppercase), and the period ('.').

These keypads are enabled by default. They can be enabled/disabled through a settings of the main TopoDroid window.
